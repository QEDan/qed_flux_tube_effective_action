✦ Investigation Summary: Discrepancy in QED Effective Action (Sech2 / Dunne-Hall)

  As of this handover, we have cleaned the codebase, removed the C backend, and verified the renormalization framework in the zero-field limit. However, a significant discrepancy (~200x) persists when validating against the Dunne-Hall exact inhomogeneous benchmark (Sech2).

  ### Resolved & Audit Findings:
  1. Backend Consolidation: Removed C backend. Only PyTorch backend remains.
  2. Dimensional Analysis Audit:
     - All core functions are now dimensionally consistent with natural units (ħ=c=1).
     - Found: `compute_uv_subtraction` was dimensionally problematic. Corrected implementation to match the functional form derived in `docs/greensfunc.tex` (Eq 2.59), utilizing a proxy field strength $B^2$ to ensure correct scaling and zero-field cancellation.
  3. Renormalization: Confirmed $G_{num} \to G_0$ in zero-field, with tests passing.

  ### Remaining Issues:
  - The ~200x discrepancy for inhomogeneous fields remains.
  - While zero-field is validated, the inhomogeneous $F \neq 0$ profiles might suffer from subtle gauge or normalization mismatches (e.g., $A_\phi$ asymptotic boundary conditions).

  ### New Hypotheses for Next Agent:
  
  #### H5: Boundary Condition Mismatch (High Priority)
  - Hypothesis: For $F \neq 0$, the exterior boundary conditions involve Bessel functions of order $n = m_l - \mathcal{F}_{ext}$. The current implementation might be using the wrong Bessel order or failing to account for the discontinuity of the derivative at the boundary correctly in the presence of flux.
  - Test: Inspect `pytorch_solver.py`'s `solve_batch` for the `lambd`-dependent jump condition logic and cross-verify Bessel orders against Section 6 of `greensfunc.tex`.

  #### H6: UV Subtraction Dimensionality (Medium Priority)
  - Hypothesis: The UV subtraction term in `renormalization.py` is currently experimental. The theoretical derivation (Eq 2.59) is complex and depends on the specific field profile flux $F$. The current $B^2$ proxy might be missing a normalization factor related to $F/\lambda^2$.
  - Test: Check the symbolic derivation of the UV term in `symbolic_validations/verify_uv_subtraction.py` against the full analytic benchmark (Step Function).

  #### H7: Spectral Summation Range (Low Priority)
  - Hypothesis: The $m_l$ summation range is insufficient for the inhomogenous profile at higher $B$.
  - Test: Increase $m_l$ range significantly in `scripts/test_sech2_exact.py` and analyze if the ratio converges.
