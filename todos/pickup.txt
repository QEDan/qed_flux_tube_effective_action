✦ Investigation Summary: Discrepancy in QED Effective Action (Sech2 / Dunne-Hall)

  As of May 2, 2026, we have established a robust internal validation suite, but a magnitude discrepancy remains between the numerical tube results and the Cartesian HE benchmark.

  ### Major Breakthroughs:
  1. **Geometry Mismatch Confirmed (H9):** Symbolically proved (`confirm_geometry_mismatch.py`) that the 2D cylindrical Sech2 tube is **not** a coordinate transformation of the 1D Cartesian Sech2 slab. Non-local effects and the centrifugal barrier $(m_l/\rho)^2$ make the Dunne-Hall integral benchmark incompatible for direct validation of the integrated action.
  2. **Gold Standard Established:** Implemented exact Whittaker-based cylindrical solutions for the **Step Function Profile** in `renormalization.py`. The numerical solver now matches this exact benchmark with $<1\%$ error (`compare_analytics.py`), proving that the solver's radial logic, measure, and normalization are correct for cylindrical tubes.
  3. **HE Benchmark Implemented:** Added exact renormalized Heisenberg-Euler and first-order derivative correction formulas to `analytic.py` as geometry-independent limits for the slowly-varying regime.

  ### Current Roadblock: Numerical Stability (NaNs)
  - When validating the Slowly-Varying limit (large $\lambda$, large domain), the solver encounters NaNs.
  - **Source:** Exponential growth/decay of radial solutions ($u'' \approx V_{eff} u$) over long domains exceeds the dynamic range of `float64`, even with periodic normalization. Specifically, Bessel initial conditions for large $m_l$ (e.g., $K_{200}(10) > 10^{300}$) overflow immediately.

  ### Recommended Next Steps:
  1. **Finalize Robust Solver:** The `PyTorchSolver` in `pytorch_solver.py` currently has a "Log-Scale RK4" implementation that needs completion. 
     - **Fix 1:** Re-integrate the `safe_bessel_bc` logic to handle Bessel order $> 100$ using power-law asymptotics to prevent initial overflow.
     - **Fix 2:** Consider the Riccati (Log-Derivative) transformation ($R = u'/u$) as a more stable alternative for the oscillatory regime.
  2. **HE Convergence Run:** Once stability is restored, run `scripts/validate_he_expansion.py` to confirm that the numerical action approaches the Heisenberg-Euler limit as $\lambda \to \infty$.
  3. **Flux Regulation:** Investigate the large imaginary parts in non-zero flux cases. This may require increasing the $m_l$ sum range further or applying the Abel-Plana regularization.
