✦ Investigation Summary: Discrepancy in QED Effective Action (Sech2 / Dunne-Hall)

  As of May 2, 2026, we have identified and corrected several fundamental errors in the Green's function solver, aligning the results with physical expectations (negative action), though a significant magnitude discrepancy (~10^6x) persists against the Dunne-Hall 1D benchmark.

  ### Critical Fixes Implemented:
  1. **Integration Measure Corrected:** The numerical Green's function $G$ is already $\rho$-scaled ($G = \rho g_{radial}$). The Orchestrator was incorrectly applying an *additional* factor of $\rho$ in the measure ($\int G \rho d\rho$). Fixed to $\int G d\rho$ to match the 3D trace $\text{Tr} G_3 \sim 2\pi \int \rho d\rho g_{radial}$.
  2. **Normalization Factor Updated:** The overall action factor was corrected to $-1/(2\pi)$. This accounts for the 4D momentum measure ($1/8\pi^2$), spin degeneracy ($2$), and the angular integral ($2\pi$).
  3. **UV Subtraction Sign Correction:** Found a sign mismatch. $G - G_0$ is negative for magnetic fields; the UV subtraction term must be **added** ($G - G_0 + UV$) to correctly cancel the negative divergence.
  4. **Global Renormalization:** Implemented a global non-oscillatory $B^2$ subtraction term in the Orchestrator: $UV_{global} = \int \rho B^2(\rho) d\rho / (6 \chi^4)$. This is required for logarithmic convergence of the $\chi$ integral in 3+1D.
  5. **Numerical Stability:** Added asymptotic approximations for $G_0$ at large $m_l$ and overflow protection for the backward ODE integration.

  ### Remaining Issues & Hypotheses:
  - **Magnitude Discrepancy:** `scripts/plot_exact_comparison.py` shows numerical results $\sim 10^7$ times larger than the derivative expansion. 
  - **Slow Spectral Convergence (H8):** The sum $\sum_{m_l} (G - G_0)$ decays very slowly with $\chi$. Truncating the sum (currently at $m_l \approx 100$) at high $\chi$ introduces a fake divergence. The number of terms needed likely scales as $m_l \sim \chi \rho_{max}$.
  - **Geometry Mismatch (H9):** The Dunne-Hall benchmark is for a 1D Cartesian profile $B(x)$. Our solver computes a 2D cylindrical profile $B(\rho)$. While the profile function is the same ($\text{sech}^2$), the transverse density of states and the area integration ($2\pi \rho d\rho$ vs $L dy$) are different. The "Analytic" results in `plot_exact_comparison.py` use a naive local area integration which may be insufficient.
  - **Aharonov-Bohm Tail (H10):** For profiles with non-zero total flux $F$, the exterior Green's function has an AB tail that only cancels in the full $m_l$ sum. This cancellation is numerically delicate and may require a more advanced regularization (e.g., Abel-Plana).

  ### Recommended Next Steps:
  1. **Convergence Study:** Use `scripts/check_term_decay.py` to plot the sum over $m_l$ vs $\chi$ and determine the actual power-law decay. If it's not $1/\chi^2$ or better, the UV cancellation is incomplete.
  2. **Small-Flux Limit:** Validate against a Zero-Flux profile ($F=0$) first, as this eliminates the AB tail issue.
  3. **Heat Kernel Audit:** Re-derive the coefficient of the global $B^2$ subtraction ($1/6$) specifically for the 1D integrated action in cylindrical coordinates.
  4. **Spectral Summations:** Consider implementing the spectral sum via the Abel-Plana formula to convert it into a stable integral.
