# Investigation Summary: Heisenberg-Euler Magnitude Discrepancy

As of May 3, 2026, the investigation into the $10^8$ magnitude discrepancy has made significant progress, though a full match remains elusive.

## Resolved Issues
1.  **Numerical Background Subtraction:** The primary source of error in zero-field cancellation was the subtraction of an analytical $G_0$ from a discretized numerical $G$. This has been replaced by computing $G_{vac}$ numerically using the same solver, resulting in machine-precision cancellation for zero-flux profiles.
2.  **Solver Stability:** Implemented a ultra-stable Riccati-based identity $G = 1/(y_0 - y_{inf})$ for the Green's function, preventing all overflow/underflow issues even for $m_l=1000$.
3.  **Action Normalization:** Corrected the global action coefficient and chi-integration measure to align with 4D QED Euclidean effective action theory.

## Current Findings
- `python scripts/test_zero_field.py` now returns `0j` (or close to machine epsilon), confirming that vacuum terms are correctly subtracted.
- Despite successful zero-field cancellation and correct step-function benchmarking, the Heisenberg-Euler validation (`scripts/validate_he_expansion.py`) still shows a massive discrepancy (factor of $10^6$-$10^8$).

## Hypotheses
1.  **Non-local vs Local:** The current benchmark is a *Local* Heisenberg-Euler Lagrangian (B^4 approximation). The numerical solver calculates the *exact* one-loop QED effective action (all orders in B). At $\lambda=5.0$, the local approximation may be fundamentally inadequate for the flux tube's non-local structure.
2.  **Spectral Sum Convergence:** Even with $m_l=\pm 300$, the convergence of the sum may be very slow for the integrated action. It might be necessary to sum higher-order terms analytically.
3.  **Renormalization/Subtraction:** There might be an additional global UV term (e.g., $B^4$ constant) required for full 4D renormalization that is currently missing in the orchestrator.
